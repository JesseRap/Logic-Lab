In creating a proof with the resolution method, this program follows two procedures: first, a procedure that transaltes an input proposition into Conjunctive Normal Form (CNF); and a procedure known as "resolve", which takes two clauses D1 and D2, where D1 contains p, and D2 contains Np, and then adds a new clause constisting of d1 "minus" p and d2 "minus" Np. In English, we take p out of one clause, and Np out of the other one, and then combine the clauses to make a new one,

Like tableaux, resolution proofs involve proof by refutation. In a proof by refutation, the argument assumes the NEGATION of the desired conclusion, along with any (optional) premises.
